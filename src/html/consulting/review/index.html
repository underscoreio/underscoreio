Review can give you assurance your team is on the right path, or highlight concerns you should be aware of.

What is code review?

Put simply, it is an independent inspection of Scala source code. Reviews focus on:
  • quality
  • maintainability
  • improvements
  • idiomatic Scala usage

What you can expect

There are four phases to the review:
  1. A conversation to understand your key concerns.
  2. The independent code review itself, leading to the Review Report.
  3. Discuss items from the report and plan the form of support required.
  4. Implement the support, which includes one day per week from our consultants, for a month.
The Review Report is structured into three sections:
  • Main findings—summarizing and explaining key concerns.
  • Recommendations—actions to take to address the findings.
  • Detailed findings—to give your team specific examples from the code base.
The initial review process takes typically two weeks from first discussion to report. All activities are on-line, unless otherwise agreed.
